Why study theory of computation

  • Computer science is fundamentally about problem solving and building general theories of the capabilities and limitations of computing. The theory of computation seeks to formalize what is solvable by computers.
  • Models of computation
    - In theory of computation you can mathematically prove whether a problem can or cannot be solved using models of computation. Models of computation are formal abstract representation of a machine that takes in an input and computes an output.
    - A turing machine is one example model of computation developed by alan turing
    The halting problem is one example of a problem that theory of computation deals with

Languages, strings, and operations

  • Two fundamental concepts in theory of computation are string and a language
  • Definition: String
    • A string is any sequence of symbols
  • Definition: Language
    • A language is a set of strings
  • We can perform operations on languages
    • Union
    • Intersection
    • Concatenation
    • Kleene star

Deterministic Finite Automaton (DFA)


Finite State machine is the simplest computational model
DFAs are 5-tuples that can be visualized with state diagrams

  • Also called a finite state automata
  • Called deterministic because the next state is always determined by input and the transition function
  • Formally defined as
    - Q is the set of states
    - is the alphabet
    - is the transition function
    - is the start state
    - F is the set of accept/final states
    Language is the set of all accepted strings, strings that result in reaching the accepted state
    A language is a regular language if it is recognized by some finite state automata

Non-deterministic Finite Automaton (NFA)

  • Another kind of automaton, called non-deterministic because for every symbol in the input the next state is not always determined.
  • NFA accepts a string if there is at least one path to an accept state
  • NFAs can also transition on the empty string . In this case no input symbol is consumed
  • Also defined using 5-tuples
  • any language recognized by a NFA is a regular language, thus any language recognized by a DFA can be recognized by a DFA
  • NFAs tend to be constructed more simply
  • produces several possible states instead of a single state

Regular Operations

  • In addition to regular languages and operation s we can define regular operations
  • Regular operations is another way of classifying the operations
  • For all regular operations the resulting language is still a regular operation. The class of regular languages is closed under the operations
  • Meaning we can always construct a Finite automaton for the resulting languages
  • Significance Given some alphabet and a small set of languages over the alphabet by applying the three regular operations we will be able to generate all the regular languages possible over that alphabet

Regular languages and model theory

The Black Technical Object by Ramon Amaro

Amaro's recent monograph, The Black Technical Object represents one of the most recent and comprehensive meditations on the intersection of Blackness, Machine Learning, and Artificial Intelligence. Amaro sets the scene and takes up the discussion of our modern Western neurosis and obsession with data as an expression of aspirational hegemonic ideal and reification and concretization of the logics of categorization. Under assumptions of race and the logics of categorization Blackness is an object ready for analysis, consumption, and algorithmic reassembly through the white imaginary. This is present in the historical legacy and ideological/metaphysical presuppositions of machine learning. He performs this analysis by investigating the historical context and evolution of machine learning from mathematics, specifically probability and statistics, alongside a philosophical critique and psychoanalytic approach to our algorithmically driven age.

On the Existence of Digital Objects by Yuk Hui

Hui argues that both in metaphysics and computation there is a lack of understanding of objects relationally because of an over reliance on substance that is inherited from Aristotelian ontology. In This book, Hui creates a conversation between Heidegger and Simondon to understand digital objects in themselves as well as the digital mileu in which they take their form. Borrowing from Floridi floridi-level-of-abstraction Hui constructs a form ground relation between ontology and ontologies. Digital objects need to be understood in terms of there structures concretized with digital representations, but also as relational objects. In addition Hui develops his mentor stiglier's idea of tiertiary rentetion to develo a kind of tiertiary protention.
